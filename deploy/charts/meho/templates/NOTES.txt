WARNING: checkRunner.enabled=true on a Vault credential backend (#2642).

  The check-runner principal mints its token with aud={{ .Values.config.keycloakAudience }}
  (the KEYCLOAK_AUDIENCE this release renders into the ConfigMap).
  The {{ .Values.config.vaultOidcRole }} JWT role this project documents binds on
  bound_audiences alone -- no bound_subject, no bound_claims -- and its
  policy grants read on all of secret/data/meho/*. Vault therefore accepts
  the runner principal against that role as-is, and background dispatch
  inherits the full policy: the "system-initiated calls cannot perform an
  operator-context Vault read" carve-out no longer holds on this release.

  If that is not what you intended, bound the role before relying on this:
  give the runner client a distinct audience INSTEAD OF the backplane one
  plus its own narrower JWT role, or add to {{ .Values.config.vaultOidcRole }} an
  exact-match bound_claims keyed on a claim value only operator tokens
  carry. A bound_claims_type=glob "*" is not a restriction -- it matches
  the runner's service-account claims too. Recipe:
  docs/cross-repo/vault-provisioning.md, "Bounding the check-runner
  principal (#2642)".

  Confirm what the role actually accepts -- an error, not a token, means
  the binding is in force:
       vault write auth/jwt/login role={{ .Values.config.vaultOidcRole }} jwt="$RUNNER_TOKEN"
